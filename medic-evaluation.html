\u25B6 Psychological Framing

This one wears their emotions on their sleeve. Said it themselves. And they are right. I can see it in the way they sit, the way they look at the extraction timer like it is counting down something more personal than seconds.

I am trying to understand the brain of a raider. Not the tactics. Not the scars. The part that decides which memories are evidence, which are punishment, and which are proof they are still human.

The problem is not that Arceology feels too much. The problem is that the surface keeps giving them reasons to stop, and they keep refusing.

Cleared for operations. But I will be watching the pauses, not the wounds.
