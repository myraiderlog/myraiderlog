DAY 101
BURIED CITY // NIGHT
SURVIVED

There he goes.

It's his raider flare… still burning as it disappears between the buildings. A thin red line dragging through the dark like the city doesn't want to let him go.

I didn't mean for it to be the last thing I saw.

We were supposed to loop back. Quiet route. Easy extraction.

He was not too far ahead of me but, just far enough that I couldn't get to him in time.

Then the Rats came out of the side streets.

I heard when the shot cracked the night.

His flare was a signal for retreat. Signal for me to live.

I ran.

I hate that part.

I have his med kit in my pack and his ammo still warm in my hands.

People say Raiders get used to loss.

They're lying.

You just get better at carrying it.

DAY 47
DAM BATTLEGROUNDS // DAWN
EXTRACTED

First time topside.

They told me it would be different. That nothing prepares you for seeing what Toledo became.

They were right.

The sun came up over Alcantara Dam and I saw the Rust Belt spread out below. Used to be beautiful, they say. Ancient Italian architecture. Now it's just bones and machines.

The cargo elevator ride up takes thirty seconds. Long enough to wonder if you'll come back down.

I made it to the dam's maintenance tunnels. Found supplies. Medical gear. Ammunition. Things Speranza needs.

Then I saw one. A Harvester. Six legs. Thirty feet tall. ARC machine moving through the ruins like it owns them.

Maybe it does.

I stayed low. Stayed quiet. Found a raider hatch before my thirty minutes ran out.

Back underground now. Safe in Speranza. But I can still hear the sound of its legs on concrete.

You don't forget that sound.

DAY 78
THE SPACEPORT // DUSK
SURVIVED

Saw another raider today at Acerra Spaceport.

We locked eyes across the tarmac. Both of us loaded with salvage. Both of us knew what came next.

Down in Speranza, maybe we'd grab a drink at Apollo's bar. Talk about our runs. Maybe even be friends.

But topside is different.

He fired first. Or maybe I did. When your heart's trying to climb out of your chest, it's hard to remember.

I won. If you can call it that.

Took his loot. Left his safe pocket items because even raiders have lines we don't cross.

Got back to Speranza and sold everything to Tian Wen. She didn't ask questions. She never does.

Later that night, I saw his name on the memorial wall in Apollo's bar. Had a family. Two kids.

I killed him for circuit boards and ARC components.

This is what we've become.

And every night, I wonder if the next name on that wall will be mine.
