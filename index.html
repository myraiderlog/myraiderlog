Who I Am // What This Is

They call me Arceology.

I don't remember when that started. Don't remember if I chose it or if it chose me. Down here, names are like everything else‚Äîfragments of who we used to be.

I should be better at this. At the distance. At the cold professionalism every other raider seems to have.

But I'm not. I feel every loss. Every close call. Every friend who didn't make it back from topside. It sits in my chest like rust on metal‚Äîcorroding, permanent, impossible to scrub away.

I wear my emotions on my sleeve. I can't help it.

// LOG.PURPOSE

This terminal... it's all I have left to process it. The runs. The grief. The small victories that feel hollow. The weight of surviving when others didn't.

Every time I go topside, I tell myself I'll be numb to it. Professional. Detached.

Every time, I come back feeling too much.

So I write. I record. I document every run like it matters. Because maybe it does. Maybe someone will read these logs and know we were here. That we tried. That we cared, even when caring made it harder to survive.

I know I shouldn't let it hit me this hard. But I do.

And maybe that's what makes me human in a world full of machines.
