!! ARC Machine Overview

ARC machines are autonomous robotic units that patrol the Rust Belt and aggressively engage any raiders they detect. They range from small aerial drones to massive armored Sentinels, each with distinct behavior patterns, weak points, and threat levels. Understanding how each machine type operates is essential for surviving raids efficiently and conserving ammunition for the encounters that matter most.

All ARC machines share a detection system based on sight, sound, and proximity. Unsuppressed weapons attract attention from farther away. Running generates more noise than walking. And every machine has a detection radius that increases if you are in its direct line of sight. Stealth is always an option against ARC machines. Not every patrol needs to be a fight.

ARC machines drop salvage components when destroyed. Higher-tier machines drop more valuable salvage. This creates a risk-reward calculation for every encounter: is the salvage worth the ammunition, health, and noise you will spend to get it?

!! Scout Drones

Recon Drone

THREAT: LOW
HP: Low
DAMAGE: None (alerts others)
WEAK POINT: Sensor lens (front)

Recon Drones are small flying units that scan the environment in sweeping patterns. They do not attack directly but will alert all nearby ARC machines to your position if they spot you. A detected Recon Drone emits a piercing alarm sound and a red scanning beam locks onto your location. Destroying the drone quickly or avoiding its scan path entirely prevents the alert. One shot from any weapon destroys a Recon Drone. The sensor lens on the front is technically the weak point but the entire unit is fragile enough that hitting it anywhere works.

Attack Drone

THREAT: MEDIUM
HP: Low-Medium
DAMAGE: Light sustained fire
WEAK POINT: Thruster unit (underside)

Attack Drones are aggressive aerial units that strafe raiders with rapid-fire energy projectiles. Their individual shots deal light damage but they fire continuously, and they typically appear in groups of two to four. The accumulated damage from multiple Attack Drones can deplete your health quickly if you stand still. The key to fighting Attack Drones is movement. Their tracking struggles with lateral strafing. Move perpendicular to their flight path while returning fire. The thruster unit on their underside takes double damage, so shooting upward at passing drones is more ammo-efficient than hitting their armored top shell.

!! Walker Units

Patrol Walker

THREAT: MEDIUM
HP: Medium
DAMAGE: Moderate burst fire
WEAK POINT: Leg joints, rear power cell

Patrol Walkers are quadrupedal machines roughly the size of a large dog. They move in pairs along set routes and engage with burst-fire weapons mounted on their backs. Their armor is moderate, requiring roughly one full magazine from an assault rifle to destroy. The leg joints are vulnerable: concentrated fire on a single leg will stagger the Walker and briefly immobilize it, creating a window to finish it off or reposition. The rear power cell is the highest-damage weak point but requires getting behind the Walker, which is risky when its partner is still active. In pairs, focus fire one Walker down completely before switching to the second.

Heavy Walker

THREAT: HIGH
HP: High
DAMAGE: High (charged beam + stomp)
WEAK POINT: Exposed core after charge attack

Heavy Walkers are large armored machines with thick plating and devastating weapons. Their primary attack is a charged beam that deals massive damage in a line, followed by a ground stomp if you are close. The beam has a visible charge-up animation with a distinct audio cue, giving you roughly two seconds to sidestep. After firing the beam, the Heavy Walker's chest plates open briefly to vent heat, exposing its core. This three-second window is your best opportunity to deal meaningful damage. Focus fire on the exposed core during this window, then get back into cover before the next attack cycle. Solo players can defeat Heavy Walkers but it takes multiple attack cycles and careful ammunition management. Squads should coordinate to chain damage windows.

!! Sentinel Class

Sentinel

THREAT: EXTREME
HP: Very High
DAMAGE: Lethal (multi-weapon platform)
WEAK POINT: Dorsal antenna array, knee joints

Sentinels are the most dangerous standard ARC machine. They stand roughly three stories tall and mount multiple weapon systems including missile pods, rotating cannons, and close-range electrical discharge. Engaging a Sentinel is a commitment, not a casual encounter. You need significant ammunition reserves, healing, and ideally a full squad to take one down efficiently.

The Sentinel cycles through attack phases. The missile barrage covers a wide area and is best avoided by sprinting perpendicular to its facing rather than away from it. The rotating cannon tracks a single target with sustained fire, dealing heavy damage per second. If targeted by the cannon, break line of sight behind solid cover immediately. The electrical discharge activates when raiders are within melee range, dealing area damage around the Sentinel's legs.

The dorsal antenna array on top of the Sentinel takes triple damage from all sources. Hitting it requires elevation or precise vertical aim when the Sentinel turns to face you. The knee joints are easier to target and stagger the Sentinel temporarily when enough damage accumulates on a single knee. A staggered Sentinel drops lower, making the antenna array accessible from ground level for several seconds.

SQUAD TACTIC Assign one player to focus exclusively on knee joints while the others deal body damage. When the Sentinel staggers, everyone switches to the antenna array for maximum damage. Two or three stagger cycles should bring a Sentinel down if your team coordinates effectively.

!! Harvesters

Harvester

THREAT: LOW (Non-aggressive)
HP: Medium
DAMAGE: Retaliatory shockwave only
WEAK POINT: Collection arm joints

Harvesters are non-combat ARC machines that collect resources from the environment. They do not attack unprovoked but will emit a shockwave that pushes raiders back and deals light damage if attacked. Destroying a Harvester yields high-value salvage components, making them attractive targets for raiders who need crafting materials. The collection arm joints are vulnerable and destroying them reduces the Harvester's retaliation capability. Harvesters that lose both collection arms cannot emit shockwaves and become completely passive. The salvage they drop is consistently valuable, making them worth hunting whenever you spot one, provided no other threats are nearby.

!! Stella Montis Boss Guide

Stella Montis is the apex ARC threat in the current Escalation season. It is a massive mobile fortress-class machine that appears in specific high-level raid zones. Engaging Stella Montis is entirely optional but completing the encounter yields the rarest crafting materials and exclusive gear in the game. This is endgame content that demands a coordinated squad, heavy ammunition investment, and thorough knowledge of its multi-phase attack patterns.

PHASE 1: PERIMETER DEFENSE (100% - 70% HP)

Stella Montis deploys waves of Attack Drones and Patrol Walkers while firing long-range artillery at raider positions. The priority in Phase 1 is clearing the deployed machines while avoiding artillery strikes, which are telegraphed by red circles on the ground roughly three seconds before impact. Do not focus damage on Stella Montis during this phase because its armor plating is fully sealed. Clear the minions, conserve resources, and wait for the transition.

PHASE 2: CORE EXPOSURE (70% - 35% HP)

At 70% HP, Stella Montis stops deploying minions and opens its hull to expose three power cores. Each core glows red and can be targeted individually. Destroying a core reduces Stella Montis's damage output and disables one of its weapon systems. The catch is that the cores are only exposed for 15 seconds at a time before the hull reseals for a cooldown period. During the cooldown, Stella Montis fires homing missiles that track individual raiders. Sprint laterally and use hard cover to break missile lock. You need to destroy all three cores across multiple exposure windows to trigger Phase 3.

PHASE 3: MELTDOWN (35% - 0% HP)

With all cores destroyed, Stella Montis enters a permanent meltdown state. Its hull stays open, exposing its central reactor to damage. However, it becomes extremely aggressive, charging directly at raiders and detonating area-of-effect shockwaves at close range. The reactor takes massive damage from all weapons. This is a DPS race: pour everything you have into the reactor while staying mobile enough to avoid the charge attacks and shockwaves. Designated healers should focus on keeping the team alive rather than dealing damage. Phase 3 typically lasts 60 to 90 seconds with a full squad outputting consistent damage.

CRITICAL Bring at least 300 rounds of primary ammunition and full healing supplies for the Stella Montis encounter. Running dry mid-fight forces you to either disengage and lose progress or die and lose everything. Preparation is everything.

!! PvP Combat Tactics

Raider-versus-raider combat in ARC Raiders is fundamentally different from fighting ARC machines. Human opponents are unpredictable, aggressive, and capable of the same tactics you use. Winning PvP encounters consistently requires a combination of mechanical skill, positional awareness, and decision-making speed.

The First-Shot Advantage

The raider who fires first wins the majority of even-skill PvP encounters. This means awareness is more important than raw aim. Constantly scan your surroundings, listen for footsteps and gunfire, and use high ground and cover to spot enemies before they spot you. If you see a raider before they see you, you control the engagement. Take your opening shot from cover at a range where your weapon is effective, and you start the fight with a significant health advantage that is very difficult for your opponent to overcome.

When to Fight vs When to Avoid

Not every raider encounter needs to be a firefight. If you have valuable loot and a clear path to extraction, avoiding combat is often the smarter play. Fights cost ammunition, health, and time, and they generate noise that attracts both ARC machines and other raiders. Engage when you have a clear advantage: better positioning, the element of surprise, or superior numbers. Disengage when the fight is not worth the risk. There is no reward for kills that do not improve your extraction odds.

Peeking and Cover Usage

Never stand in the open during a firefight. Use cover aggressively: peek out to fire, then return to cover before the enemy can retaliate accurately. Quick peeks from alternating sides of cover make you difficult to track. Slow, predictable peeking gets you headshot. Mix up your timing and your angles. If an enemy is holding a strong cover position, do not repeat-peek the same angle. Either reposition to a different angle or use throwables to force them out.

Disengagement

Knowing when and how to disengage saves more raids than winning fights. If a fight is going badly, break line of sight immediately. Sprint behind hard cover, change direction, and put distance between yourself and the engagement. Most raiders will not chase a fleeing opponent for more than a few seconds because the pursuit exposes them to third-party attacks. Smoke grenades, flashbangs, and noise decoys all create disengagement opportunities. Healing during a disengage and re-engaging from a different angle can turn a losing fight into a winning one.

!! Positioning & Engagement Rules

Positioning is the single most influential factor in combat outcomes, above weapon choice, above aim skill, above gear quality. A raider with an average weapon in a superior position beats a raider with a perfect loadout caught in the open. Internalize these principles and apply them to every encounter.

Always fight from elevation when possible. High ground gives you better sightlines, makes you harder to push, and forces enemies to expose themselves when engaging upward. If you cannot get elevation, fight from hard cover that protects your body while allowing you to shoot.

Never fight in a position you cannot retreat from. Before engaging, identify your escape route. If the fight goes wrong, you need to be able to break contact and reach safety without crossing open ground under fire.

Control engagement distance. If you have a long-range weapon, keep distance. If you have a shotgun, close distance before firing. Fighting outside your weapon's effective range is the most common mistake in PvP combat. Reposition to your weapon's preferred range before committing to the engagement.

ARCEOLOGIST'S NOTE Combat awareness comes from experience. Every fight, win or lose, teaches you something about positioning, timing, and weapon handling. Review your deaths honestly. The raider who learns from every loss improves faster than the one who blames their weapons.
